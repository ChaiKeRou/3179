CAUSE OF DEATH ACROSS AGE & GENDER

In 2022, Malaysia recorded a total of 206,525 deaths, with 117,790 males and 88,735 females. Although males have higher mortality rates across most age groups, females surpass males in the 85+ age category.

Among age groups over 60, Ischaemic Heart Diseases and Pneumonia were the leading causes of death for both males and females, accounting for 8,144 and 5,857 deaths, respectively.

TRENDS IN CAUSES OF DEATH LEADING TO 2022

Since 2018, the Ministry of Health Malaysia (MoH) has implemented a process to verify non-medically certified causes of death to increase percentage of deaths that are medically certified. In 2022, 61.1% of 206,525 recorded deaths were medically certified, while 38.9% were not.

The Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM) follows ICD-10, published by the World Health Organization, to categorize causes of death. These causes are classified as Medically Certified, where verification is done by medical officers or coroners, and Non-medically Certified, where verification is conducted by informants without medical qualifications, such as individuals or the police.
