CAUSE OF DEATH

IN MALAYSIA, 2022

Statistics on Causes of Death, Malaysia, 2023 presents data on the leading causes of death in 2022. The Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM) classifies these causes using the ICD-10 system by the World Health Organization (2010). The data, sourced from the National Registration Department, reflect demographic adjustments from the Population and Housing Census of Malaysia 2020 (MyCensus 2020) for the years 2020 to 2022.

Causes of death are categorized as either medically or non-medically certified:

  • Medically certified: Verified by medical officers or coroners.
  • Non-medically certified: Verified by individuals without medical qualifications, such as informants or police.

Since 2018, the Ministry of Health Malaysia (MoH) has worked to increase medically certified deaths. In 2022, 61.1% of 206,525 deaths were medically certified, while 38.9% were non-medically certified. This report highlights the leading medically certified causes of death in 2022.

In 2022, Malaysia recorded a total of 206,525 deaths, with 117,790 males and 88,735 females, indicating a significant gender disparity in mortality rates, where male deaths exceeded female deaths by 14.07%.

In middle age, deaths sharply increased from 4,632 in the 35-39 years group to 11,865 in the 50-54 years group, with males consistently exhibiting higher mortality rates. Among the elderly (60 years and above), the 85 and over group recorded the highest mortality at 27,735 deaths, with females (17,085) outnumbering males (10,650). In contrast, in the 70-74 years age group, males slightly outnumbered females, with 14,446 deaths compared to 10,543 females.

Overall, while males have higher mortality rates in most age groups, females outnumber males in the 85+ category, highlighting significant changes in mortality patterns with age.
